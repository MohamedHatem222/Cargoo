Dispatcher:

A truck dispatcher is a professional responsible for coordinating and managing the movement of trucks and drivers to ensure efficient transportation operations. They work closely with truck drivers, shippers, and customers to plan and schedule deliveries, track shipments, and handle any issues or delays that may arise during the transportation process. Truck dispatchers typically use communication tools, such as phones, emails and computer systems, to stay in contact with drivers and provide them with instructions and updates. They play a crucial role in optimizing routes, managing logistics, and ensuring timely and safe delivery of goods.

Dispatcher Requirements:

  • - Have logistics background
  • - Must be able to work from home 6AM-5PM U.S central time
  • - Must have access to an updated computer and fast internet.

Compliance and safety:

The compliance and safety officer in a trucking company is responsible for ensuring the company's adherence to transportation laws and safety standards. They oversee compliance with regulations, develop and implement safety programs, and provide training for drivers and employees. Maintaining records related to safety and compliance, they monitor driver hours, vehicle maintenance, and equipment inspections. They collaborate with government agencies during inspections and investigations, aiming to prevent accidents and improve safety practices.
